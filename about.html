About Our Church

G.C.C.M (Glory of Christ Church Ministries) Church is a vibrant Christian congregation located in Kimaeti, a village in Bungoma County, Kenya. The church is well-known for its community-centered mission, focusing on spiritual growth and social welfare. It offers a welcoming environment for people of all ages and backgrounds, emphasizing the teachings of Jesus Christ through dynamic worship, preaching, and Bible study sessions. Led by a dedicated pastoral team, G.C.C.M Church regularly engages in outreach programs aimed at improving the lives of the local community. These initiatives include food distribution to vulnerable families, educational support for underprivileged children, and youth mentorship programs. The church also organizes prayer meetings, fellowship groups, and charity events to foster unity and support among its members. The congregation is known for its lively Sunday services, featuring uplifting music and powerful sermons that encourage faith and hope. G.C.C.M Church places a strong emphasis on family, encouraging members to build strong spiritual and moral foundations. Through its commitment to the gospel and community service, the church has become a beacon of hope and transformation in Kimaeti and beyond.

Servises Organisation

G.C.C.M Church in Kimaeti organizes its services to cater to different spiritual needs and to encourage active participation from the congregation. Here is a general structure of how they have organized their services:

Sunday Service:

  • Morning Worship: Typically begins with vibrant praise and worship, which includes singing, dancing, and music led by the church choir and worship team.
  • Sermon: After worship, a powerful sermon is delivered by the pastor or a guest preacher, focusing on biblical teachings, faith, and Christian living.
  • Prayer Sessions: The service includes moments for personal prayers, intercessory prayers, and blessings.
  • Children’s Ministry: During Sunday services, there’s usually a special session for children where they are taught Bible lessons in an interactive and age-appropriate manner.
  • Offerings and Tithes: Members are encouraged to give offerings and tithes as part of their worship.

    • Midweek Bible Study and Prayer Meetings:

  • Held in the evenings, these sessions focus on in-depth Bible teaching and corporate prayer. Members gather to study scripture together, ask questions, and engage in prayer for various needs. Youth and Women’s Fellowship:
  • Youth services and women’s fellowship meetings are organized separately, usually on Saturdays or special days, focusing on issues relevant to these groups. This structure ensures that all members are spiritually nourished and remain engaged throughout the week.
